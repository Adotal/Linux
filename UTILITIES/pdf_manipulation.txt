0. GhostScript
COMPRESS PDF

$ gs -sDEVICE=pdfwrite -dCompatibilityLevel=1.4 -dPDFSETTINGS=/ebook -dNOPAUSE -dQUIET -dBATCH -sOutputFile=output.pdf input.pdf

Summary of -dPDFSETTINGS:

    /screen		- lower quality, smaller size. (72 dpi)
    /ebook		- for better quality, but slightly larger pdfs. (150 dpi)
    /prepress	- output similar to Acrobat Distiller "Prepress Optimized" setting (300 dpi)
    /printer 	- selects output similar to the Acrobat Distiller "Print Optimized" setting (300 dpi)
    /default 	- selects output intended to be useful across a wide variety of uses, possibly at the expense of a larger output file

1. qpdf

It is probably the most robust tool for transforming PDFs without losing quality. qpdf is ideal for merging, splitting, encrypting, and optimizing files.

COMPRESS PDF

$ qpdf --compress-streams=y --recompress-flate --optimize-images input.pdf output.pdf

MERGE FILES

$ qpdf --empty --pages file1.pdf file2.pdf -- output.pdf

SPLIT FILES (extract oages 1-5):

$ qpdf input.pdf --pages . 1-5 -- output.pdf

DELETE PASSWORD

$ qpdf --decrypt input.pdf output.pdf

LINEARIZE (fast web-view)

$ qpdf --linearize input.pdf output.pdf. 

2. OCRmyPDF
If you have a PDF that is just an image and you want the text to be selectable (OCR), OCRmyPDF is the gold standard on Linux.

MAKE PDF SEARCHABLE

$ ocrmypdf input.pdf output.pdf

OPTIMIZE AND CLEAN SCANS

$ ocrmypdf --optimize 3 --deskew input.pdf output.pdf

3. img2pdf
img2pdf is superior to any other tool because it does not re-encode the image, maintaining the exact original quality.

USE

$ img2pdf *.jpg -o portfolio.pdf
